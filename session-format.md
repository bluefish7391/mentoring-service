# Creative Tech Lab — Session Format

## Philosophy
- "Buffet" approach: expose students to a wide variety of CS topics they wouldn't encounter in school
- Goal is curiosity and discovery, not mastery or credentials
- Sessions are fully self-contained and modular — no prerequisites, no makeup work
- Low-stakes, fun-first, hands-on throughout

## Actual Session Structure (from Cryptocurrency lesson plan)
Sessions are NOT a rigid 3-step framework. They are a continuous, evolving narrative led by mentors:
1. **Welcome & intro** — icebreaker, quote of the day, topic framing (~10 min)
2. **Simulation/activity** — the entire session IS the activity; students play a game or simulation whose rules evolve incrementally to introduce CS concepts (e.g., economy game → fraud → digital signatures → blockchain → miners → proof of work)
3. **Conclusion** — recap key concepts, tease next topics (~5 min)

There is no separate "personalized project" build phase. The activity itself is collaborative and instructor-guided throughout. Students leave with conceptual understanding and experiential memory and sometimes with a known artifact.

## Topics covered (known)
- Cryptocurrency / Bitcoin (blockchain, digital signatures, proof of work, decentralization)
- Networking (internet infrastructure simulation)
- Cryptography (Enigma, digital signatures)
